Aloo Gobi

Description

Aloo gobi is a classic vegetarian dish from the Indian subcontinent, combining potatoes (aloo) and cauliflower (gobhi) in a medley of aromatic spices. Originating in the Punjab region, it is now a staple across India, Pakistan, and Nepal. It is widely considered a comfort food, appreciated for its warm, hearty, and flavorful nature.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon minced garlic
  • 1 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • ½ teaspoon ground turmeric
  • ½ teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • ½ teaspoon garam masala
  • salt to taste
  • 1 pound cauliflower
  • 1 teaspoon chopped fresh cilantro

Steps

Step 1:
Put the oil in a medium skillet over medium heat. Stir in the cumin seeds, garlic, and ginger paste. Cook about 1 minute until garlic is lightly browned. Add the potatoes. Season with turmeric, paprika, cumin, garam masala, and salt. Cover and continue cooking 5 to 7 minutes stirring occasionally.

Step 2:
the cauliflower and cilantro into the saucepan. Reduce heat to low and cover. Stirring occasionally, continue cooking 10 minutes, or until potatoes and cauliflower are tender.
